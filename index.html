Abstract

Large language models (LLMs) have improved significantly in their reasoning through extensive training on massive datasets. However, relying solely on additional data for improvement is becoming increasingly impractical, highlighting the need for models to autonomously enhance their reasoning without external supervision. In this paper, we propose Debate, Train, Evolve (DTE), a novel ground truth-free training framework that uses multi-agent debate traces to evolve a single language model. We also introduce a new prompting strategy Reflect-Critique-Refine, to improve debate quality by explicitly instructing agents to critique and refine their reasoning.

Extensive evaluations on five reasoning benchmarks with six open-weight models show that our DTE framework achieve substantial improvements, with an average accuracy gain of 8.92% on the challenging GSM-PLUS dataset. Furthermore, we observe strong cross-domain generalization, with an average accuracy gain of 5.8% on all other benchmarks, suggesting that our method captures general reasoning capabilities.

Our framework code and trained models will be publicly available upon publication.

Key Findings

Breakthrough insights from our research that advance the field of autonomous language model reasoning

Self-Evolution Works

8.92% Average Gain

Our DTE framework enables models to autonomously improve their reasoning capabilities through multi-agent debate traces, achieving substantial accuracy improvements across challenging mathematical reasoning tasks without additional supervision.

RCR Prompting Excellence

50% Sycophancy Reduction

Our Reflect-Critique-Refine prompting strategy explicitly forces agents to identify, critique, and correct reasoning errors, halving the sycophancy rate from 0.28 to 0.13 and improving debate quality significantly.

Cross-Domain Generalization

5.8% Average Improvement

Models evolved on one dataset (e.g., GSM8K) show consistent improvements on other reasoning tasks, suggesting that DTE captures general reasoning heuristics rather than dataset-specific patterns.

Single-Model Efficiency

Multi-Agent Benefits

DTE successfully distills the reasoning insights from multi-agent debate into efficient single-model inference, eliminating the computational overhead of running multiple models at inference time.

GRPO Superiority

Best Training Method

Group Relative Policy Optimization consistently outperforms supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and Direct Preference Optimization (DPO), providing better reward balancing and policy stability while maintaining low KL divergence.

Temperature Annealing

1/3 KL Drift Reduction

For smaller models (<3B parameters), linear temperature decay from 0.7 to 0.3 across evolution rounds reduces KL drift by 1/3 and recovers up to 76% of performance loss, preventing model collapse.
